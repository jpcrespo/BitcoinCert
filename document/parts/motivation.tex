\section{Motivation}

The motivation for this work is the fragility of centralized verification models.
If an issuing organization disappears, the verifiability of its documents is lost.
The reliability of a decentralized network lies in its consensus protocol.
Bitcoin is based on Proof of Work (PoW), making it a secure, immutable, and denial-of-service (DoS) resistant network.

These characteristics are not as strong or are not present in other networks.
Solutions in document verification utilize other networks, such as Ethereum, due to the versatility of their Turing-complete scripts and their different scalability trade-offs compared to Bitcoin.

With Taproot, security and blockspace efficiency are improved.
This enables new applications on the main layer (layer 1).
Our proposal does not require another protocol upgrade, nor does it resort to a second-layer blockchain or networks other than Bitcoin.
